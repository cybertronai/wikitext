## Status

## Live evidence that the v1 FF stage learned nothing

From `submissions/ff_pretrain_then_sgd/run.log`, Stage 1 telemetry (excerpt):

```
[ff] step    1  g_pos=[381.0 381.0 381.0 381.0 381.0 381.0]  g_neg=[381.0 381.0 381.0 381.0 381.0 381.0]
[ff] step  100  g_pos=[384.4 385.4 385.8 386.2 392.2 398.3]  g_neg=[384.4 385.3 385.7 386.1 392.1 398.2]
[ff] step  300  g_pos=[384.9 386.3 386.4 386.4 385.7 382.9]  g_neg=[383.9 385.3 385.4 385.4 384.7 382.1]
[ff_pretrain] gate ratio (post/init) per block: [1.01 1.01 1.01 1.00 1.00 1.00]
```

`g_pos` and `g_neg` are within 1 unit of each other at every layer at
every logged step. The FF loss
`softplus(-(g_pos - θ)) + softplus(g_neg - θ)` with θ = 384 receives
essentially identical positive and negative goodness — the gradient signal
is *the difference of two near-equal numbers*, which is numerical noise.
`h_std` stays at 1.00 ± 0.01 across all 6 blocks, 300 steps. The "sanity
gate PASSED" line is misleading: it passed because the gate ratio
threshold (5.0) was vacuously above 1.01, not because FF taught the model
anything.

**The PASS we are seeing at 0.708 is `modded_nanogpt` with 1900/2150 Stage-2
steps, minus 13 s of Stage-1 wall-clock that updated nothing.** The
hypothesis "FF gives a better init" is empirically refuted on this run.

## What v1 got wrong (root-cause)

1. **Critical (vindicated by live data): random-byte-corrupt negatives are
   trivially noise.** Replacing the last byte of a context window with a
   uniform random byte yields a "negative" example that differs from the
   positive only at one position out of 256. The model's goodness function
   (sum of squared activations over a transformer block) is dominated by
   the first 255 shared positions; the single-position perturbation moves
   `||h||²` by ~1/256 of its total. With θ = `model_dim` = 384, the
   margin is dwarfed by signal magnitude. Hinton 2022 used **plausible
   negatives generated by a separate generative model** for exactly this
   reason. v1 uses a pathological negative construction.

2. **Critical (vindicated by 12% eval): wall-clock confound.** v1 budgets
   80 s for FF and 215 s for Stage 2; Stage 2 ran 1900 steps vs the
   baseline's 2150 (12% fewer). At 0.708 acc, the result is
   indistinguishable from "modded_nanogpt at 88% step count", which is
   exactly what my v1 cross-check predicted ("Stage 2 alone is just
   truncated baseline").

3. **Moderate: θ = `model_dim` is the wrong magnitude for transformer
   goodness.** Hinton's θ = N is for an MLP with N neurons in a layer.
   Transformer block goodness is summed over (T × d) positions; the
   correct θ scales with T. v1's `θ = 384` is ~T = 1024× too small,
   pushing all positives and negatives into the softplus saturation
   regime, which is what produces the ~381 plateau in the live data.

## v2 design

Discard random-byte-corrupt FF. Replace with **Mono-Forward** (per-layer
cross-entropy on a probe head, Gong, Li, Abdulla 2025 arXiv 2501.09238) as
the primary, with **CwC** (channel-wise competitive FF, Papachristodoulou
et al. AAAI 2024 arXiv 2312.12668) as the fallback. Both eliminate
negative-sample construction entirely.

### Axis A — Mono-Forward (primary)

For each transformer block ℓ, add a small linear probe head
`H_ℓ : R^d → R^256` that predicts the next byte from the block's output.
Stage 1 trains each `H_ℓ` independently with standard cross-entropy on
detached block outputs:

```python
for step in range(stage1_steps):
    x, y = sample_batch(...)
    h = embed(x)
    for ell, block in enumerate(blocks):
        h = block(h)
        with torch.no_grad():
            h_det = h.detach()
        logits_ell = probe_heads[ell](h_det)             # block-local
        loss_ell = F.cross_entropy(logits_ell.flatten(0, 1), y.flatten())
        loss_ell.backward()                              # ONLY on block ℓ + H_ℓ
        # Per-block optimizer step
        opts_block[ell].step(); opts_block[ell].zero_grad()
        # h is detached for ℓ+1's input — no cross-block flow
        h = h.detach().clone().requires_grad_()
    # body has been trained block-locally
```

Key properties:

- No global backward chain; each block trains against its own probe head's
  loss. Gradient stops at the block boundary.
- Standard cross-entropy gives a *bounded, well-scaled* loss; no goodness
  saturation issue.
- No negative samples to construct. The "positive" and "negative" structure
  is absorbed into the probabilistic next-byte target.
- Probe heads `H_ℓ` are discarded at the end of Stage 1; only the body
  weights (which received block-local gradient) carry forward to Stage 2.

Stage 2: same as v1 — full backprop SGD with cross-entropy on the final
head. Use **matched step count to baseline (2150 steps)**, not v1's 1900.
This requires shrinking Stage 1 budget.

### Axis B — budget

- Stage 1: 30 s (down from v1's 80 s). At ~25 ms per block-local step,
  6 blocks each get ~200 update steps before the body is handed to Stage 2.
- Stage 2: 250 s, with `n_steps = 2150` (matching baseline). This isolates
  "did Stage 1 produce a better init?" from "did Stage 2 have less time?"

Total: 280 s wall + 20 s margin. Energy projection: ~48–50 kJ (Stage 1 is
cheaper than Stage 2 per-step; should net out roughly even with baseline).

### Axis C — diagnostics that would have caught v1's failure

Mandatory Stage-1 diagnostic gate before proceeding to Stage 2:

```python
# Run a 200-char val probe at end of Stage 1 using each probe head's
# logits (averaging across blocks). If acc < 0.10 (better than uniform
# = 1/256 = 0.004), Stage 1 actually produced predictive features.
stage1_val_acc = quick_eval(probe_heads, val_chars=200)
print(f"[stage1] probe-ensemble val_acc = {stage1_val_acc:.4f}")
if stage1_val_acc < 0.10:
    print("[stage1] FAILED — probe heads produced no signal; abort gracefully")
    return modded_nanogpt_baseline_path(...)
```

v1's sanity gate ("gate ratio < 5.0") was structured to **pass even when
FF did nothing** — exactly what happened. The Mono-Forward gate above
fails fast if the per-block probes are no better than chance.

### Hypothesis (revised)

- Mono-Forward Stage 1 leaves the body with hidden states that are
  *immediately* useful — `stage1_val_acc` will land in the 0.15–0.30 range
  (well above uniform).
- Stage 2 with matched-2150 step count + Mono-Forward init lands acc
  ≥ 0.738 at energy ≤ 50 kJ — strictly Pareto-improving over baseline if
  the FF-style block-local pretrain has any value at all.
- If Stage-2 result is within ±0.003 of baseline modded_nanogpt at matched
  steps, the conclusion is "block-local pretrain is wall-clock-neutral and
  acc-neutral" — useful negative.

## Success criteria

- **Strong PASS**: val ≥ 0.74 at energy ≤ 48 kJ. First block-local
  pretrained char-LM Pareto-beats modded_nanogpt.
- **Weak PASS**: val ≥ 0.70 at any energy ≤ 50 kJ, with
  `stage1_val_acc ≥ 0.10` confirming Stage 1 actually trained.
- **Refutation**: `stage1_val_acc < 0.10` — block-local CE doesn't produce
  predictive features at this scale. Abort gracefully.
- **Confound-controlled negative**: matched-step Stage 2 lands within
  ±0.003 of baseline → block-local pretrain doesn't help, doesn't hurt.

## Failure modes & diagnostics

- **Probe-head capacity dominates per-block signal**: probe heads are
  `Linear(d, 256)` — small enough that signal must come from the block,
  not the probe. Cross-check by also training a probe head from the
  *embedding output* (block 0 input); if `stage1_val_acc[probe_emb] ≈
  stage1_val_acc[probe_block_5]`, probes alone are doing the work.
- **Stage 1 spends budget on under-trained blocks**: budget per block is
  ~5 s = ~200 steps at the smaller Stage-1 batch. Verify by logging
  per-block per-step gradient norms; if `||∇block_ell||` is at chance
  noise at step 100, the block is not learning.
- **Discrepancy between probe-head-trained body and Stage-2 head-trained
  body**: catastrophic forgetting risk in Stage 2 when the body sees the
  full-graph backward. Mitigate by initializing Stage 2's optimizer
  with a small lr ramp.
